Slide 3 · Data 1 — Dashboard Insights

What the Data Reveals

The baseline risk story is not just high AI use. It is high use combined with weak verification, visible physical strain, and a smaller subgroup with much sharper dependence signals.

1 · Verification drops as task complexity rises
Low 66.0% Medium 53.6% High 40.4%
  • Shows: students verify less when outputs are harder to judge.
  • Why it matters: the weakest checking behavior appears in the highest-risk work.
2 · Over-reliant students are the clearest intervention target
23 dominant over-reliant students
36.0% verification rate
68.4% accept without checking
241 → 306s latency with vs without AI
  • Shows: this subgroup verifies least and slows down sharply without AI.
  • Why it matters: Eastbrook can prioritize targeted intervention instead of broad reminders.
3 · Physical strain is already severe at baseline
Screen time 8.6 hrs/day Eye dryness 7.1/10 Neck pain 5.9/10
  • Shows: device-linked strain is a main pattern, not a side finding.
  • Why it matters: the solution needs health guardrails as well as academic prompts.
4 · Social-emotional AI use adds safeguarding risk
Social messaging 41.6% Serious topics 33.2% PII shared 13.6%
  • Shows: some students use AI beyond assignment support.
  • Why it matters: educators need visibility into where academic and well-being risks overlap.
Age interpretation

Both age groups show heavy exposure. Older students are slightly higher, but the stronger story is school-wide overuse.

How the Prototype Supports Decision-Making

Review roster risk, open alerts, send nudges, and monitor trend summaries before risk becomes normal.
