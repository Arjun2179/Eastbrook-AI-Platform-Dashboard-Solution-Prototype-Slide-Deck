# Speaker Notes

## Slide 1

- Start with the core claim: Eastbrook is not facing just “more AI use,” but a linked verification and well-being problem.
- Point out the 4 anchor numbers:
  - `14,119 prompts/day`
  - `56.0% verification`
  - `8.6 hrs/day`
  - `23` dominant over-reliant students
- Mention that the project does not propose banning AI. It proposes safer use with earlier visibility.

## Slide 2

- Explain that the original dashboard is the **AS-IS** story only.
- Call out that the dashboard covers `Days 1–15` and `6,000` rows.
- Explain that the filters help test whether the issue is isolated or system-wide.

## Slide 3

- Keep the insight story tight:
  - harder tasks -> less verification
  - over-reliant subgroup -> highest risk
  - physical strain -> already visible
  - social-emotional use -> broadens the risk beyond homework
- Avoid sounding like you are reading from a report.

## Slide 4

- Frame the app as the proposed solution, not as a finished district-wide deployment.
- Explain the role logic:
  - students see and log behavior
  - educators get early warning and intervention tools
  - analysts test whether the intervention improves the KPIs
- Explicitly mention the assignment note:
  - no login page is required for the prototype

## Slide 5

- Explain that TO-BE is the intervention-phase or simulated-improvement comparison.
- Use the comparison to show that the intervention changes behavior, not just presentation.
- Safe closing statement:
  - the comparison suggests that adding friction and visibility improves verification and slightly reduces physical strain without removing AI from the workflow
